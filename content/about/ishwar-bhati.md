  bio: |
    Ishwar Bhati's professional career in sports began in 2006 at St. Mary's Public School and Delhi Public School, Vasant Kunj — two years before he joined ITL Public School, Dwarka, where he has now taught Physical Education for 18 consecutive years. The court at ITL is not just where he works; it is where IB Sports Academy was founded in 2010 and where it still trains today.

    Over 20 years in sport, one school, one court. That continuity is rare anywhere. In Delhi, it is unique. He built the academy not as a business, but as an answer to a question he saw every day: **what happens to the talented kid after school PE ends?**

    While basketball is his core expertise, his programme spans the full spectrum of school sport. He has organised <span class="text-primary">**Zonal Tournaments in Basketball, Football, and Cricket for the Directorate of Education**</span>, overseeing competitions across 50+ Delhi schools. He coordinated India's first international Wheelchair Cricket match between India and Bangladesh, and has been an active organiser of Khelo India events. IB Sports is not a basketball academy. It is a sports institution.
