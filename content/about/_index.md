origin:
  enable: true
  subtitle: "Since 2010 · Dwarka, Delhi"
  title: "It Started Where the Passion Already Lived"
  image: "images2/about/origin-itl-public-school-dwarka-ib-sports-academy.jpg"
  description: |
    The court was already there. So was the coach.

    Ishwar Bhati had been a Physical Education teacher at **ITL Public School, Dwarka Sector 9** long before 2010 — teaching basketball, developing athletes, building what would become something far larger. For years it happened within the natural boundary of school PE: classes, students, a court, a season.

    But somewhere along the way, the boundary stopped being enough.

    The students coming through his classes were talented — some genuinely exceptional. And Ishwar could see clearly what was missing: structured coaching beyond the school bell, year-round training, international exposure — the kind that changes how a young athlete sees themselves and what they believe is possible.

    So in 2010, PE classes became something more deliberate. A coaching academy. A formal structure. A commitment that the court at ITL would be the starting point, not the limit.

    **Ishwar still teaches Physical Education at ITL Public School today.** The academy runs across 9+ locations — but the court where it all began remains his home ground. That continuity is not accidental. It is the whole point.

why_basketball:
  enable: true
  subtitle: "Why Basketball"
  title: "India's Most Underrated Sport Has Been Waiting Its Turn"
  body: |
    Cricket has decades of infrastructure and cultural momentum. Football is finding its audience. We celebrate every sport that captures a child's imagination.

    But basketball has quietly been waiting its turn.

    Consider what the sport actually is — one of the fastest formats in competitive sport. A game where each team gets possession alternately, meaning the momentum never stays in one place for long. Physically demanding, tactically deep, and accessible: all you need is a ball and a court.

    **The sport has everything. What it has lacked in India is not talent — it is opportunity.** No visible pathway from a school court to a career. The infrastructure that turns a sport into a movement was simply never assembled for basketball the way it was for other sports. An entire generation of potential players grew up without knowing what they might have been — not because they lacked ability, but because nobody opened the door.

    That is what IB Sports Academy exists to change. We are not in competition with any other sport. We are simply opening a door that should have been open a long time ago.
